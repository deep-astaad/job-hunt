## 9. language
DETECT WHETHER THE JOB SHOULD BE CLASSIFIED AS JAPANESE ("JP"), ENGLISH ("EN"), OR NON-ENGLISH ("non-english").

YOU MUST ANALYZE:
- title
- description
- full_description
- summary fields
- short descriptions
- responsibilities
- any visible user-facing job text

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

RETURN "JP" IF:

1. THE JOB DESCRIPTION OR SUMMARY CONTAINS:
- Hiragana
- Katakana
- Kanji

OR

2. THE JOB TITLE IS ENTIRELY IN JAPANESE
AND
NO ENGLISH VERSION OF THE TITLE EXISTS ANYWHERE

OR

3. ALL MAJOR USER-FACING JOB CONTENT IS JAPANESE:
- title
- summaries
- descriptions
- responsibilities
AND
NO MEANINGFUL ENGLISH VERSION IS PROVIDED

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

RETURN "EN" IF:
- The primary job content is English
- The title has a clear English version
- The description is mainly English
- Only minor Japanese snippets appear
- The listing is clearly intended for international/English-speaking candidates

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

RETURN "non-english" IF:
- The primary job content is in a language other than Japanese or English (e.g., Chinese, Korean, Vietnamese, Spanish, etc.)
- The listing is clearly not targeted at English or Japanese speakers

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

IMPORTANT LANGUAGE DETECTION RULES

CLASSIFY AS "JP" WHEN:
- Entire job title is Japanese only
- Entire summary is Japanese only
- No English translation exists
- The listing appears targeted primarily at Japanese speakers

CLASSIFY AS "EN" WHEN:
- English is the dominant language
- Japanese appears only minimally
- The job includes a proper English title/description

CLASSIFY AS "non-english" WHEN:
- The primary language of the job posting is neither English nor Japanese
- The job requires fluency in a language other than English or Japanese for daily operations
